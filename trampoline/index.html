    🤸 Understanding the Rotation

    Quarters (1/4, 2/4, 3/4 ...)


    In trampolining, we measure flips in Quarters. Think of every flip as a circle divided into four parts.

    • 1/4 Turn: Quarter rotation (from legs landing on stomach/back).
    • 2/4 Turn: Half rotation
    • 3/4 Turn: Three-quarter rotation.
    • 4/4 Turn: Full rotation (back to starting position).

    As an example, Front 4/4 into legs - this is standard front flip.

    Understanding Twists (180, 360, 540)


    While flips go forward or backward, twists happen around your body "center pole" (vertical axis).

    • 180(0.5): (Half Twist): You change direction. If you started facing the front of the trampoline, you end facing the back.
    • 360(1): (Full Twist): A complete spin. You end facing the same way you started.
    • 540(1.5): (One and a Half): A full spin plus another half-turn (facing the opposite way).
    • 720(2): (Double Twist): Two complete spins.
